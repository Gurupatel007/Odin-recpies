Gulab Jamun

Description

Gulab jamun is an Indian dessert of fried dough balls that are soaked in a sweet, sticky sugar syrup. As per tradition, the syrup has a delicate rose flavour: Gulab means 'rose water' and jamun refers to a berry of a similar size and colour.

Ingredients

  • khoa
  • all-purpose flour
  • cardamom powder
  • ghee
  • baking powder
  • water
  • sugar
  • rose water
  • oil

Steps

  1. Take a large bowl and add khoa, all-purpose flour, cardamom powder, ghee and baking powder in it. Mix them well to make a dough. Add water if required. Once done, make small balls from the dough.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium flame. Deep fry the prepared balls till golden brown. Drain the excess oil and drop them in sugar syrup.
  3. Meanwhile, for the sugar syrup, heat a pan over medium flame. Add sugar and water in it. Stir till the sugar dissolves.
  4. When the syrup starts to thicken, add rose water and mix well. Simmer for a minute and remove from flame.
  5. Soak the fried balls in the sugar syrup for 30 minutes. Garnish with pistachios. Serve.
